### Aaj ki kahani (page ka khulasa)

#### Hissa A — Block 1: Mausoolah ka practical (5 exercise jumlay)

Section 15 (p-20) par Mausoolah ki 10 forms paradigm + Sila ki tareef + 4-layer Tarkeeb walkthrough mila tha. Aaj **5 practice jumlay** dete hain — Nazir ko tarkeeb karna:

1. **جَاءَ الَّذِيْ ضَرَبَكَ** — "Wo aaya jis ne tujhe maara"
   - **الَّذِيْ** (Mausool, m sing) + **ضَرَبَكَ** (Sila — Jumla Fi'liya containing return-pronoun كَ)
   - Mausool+Sila mil kar **Fa'il** of جَاءَ
2. **رَأَیْتُ الَّذِيْ ضَرَبَكَ** — "Main ne us shakhs ko dekha jis ne tujhe maara"
   - Same Mausool+Sila as #1, magar outer role **Maf'ool of رَأَیْتُ**
3. **مَرَرْتُ بِالَّذِيْنَ ضَرَبُوْكَ** — "Main un sab logon ke paas se guzra jinhone tujhe maara"
   - **الَّذِيْنَ** (Mausool, m plural) + **ضَرَبُوْكَ** (Sila — plural verb with return-pronoun كَ)
   - **بِ** (Harf-e-Jarr) lagi hui — Mausool+Sila Majroor role mein
4. **أَكْرِمْ مَنْ أَكْرَمَكَ** — "Tu izzat de us ko jis ne tujhe izzat di"
   - **مَنْ** (Mausool — human generic) + **أَكْرَمَكَ** (Sila — return-pronoun كَ)
   - **أَكْرِمْ** = Fi'l Amr; Mausool+Sila = Maf'ool
5. **قَرَأْتُ مَا کَتَبْتَ** — "Main ne wo padha jo tu ne likha"
   - **مَا** (Mausool — non-human generic) + **کَتَبْتَ** (Sila)
   - **قَرَأْتُ** = Fi'l Maazi + ضمیر متصل; Mausool+Sila = Maf'ool

**Pedagogical pattern** *(meri taraf se observation)*: 5 jumlay dikhate hain ke **Mausool+Sila ka complete unit** alag-alag grammatical roles nibha sakta — Fa'il (#1), Maf'ool (#2, #4, #5), Majroor (#3). **Inner Mausool+Sila ki nature constant** rehti, magar **outer role badalti**. Yeh Section 11 ke Zayd 3-form i'rab demo ka extension hai — wahaan Zayd (akela ism) ne 3 roles dikhayi thi, yahaan complete Mausool+Sila unit ne 3 roles.

#### Hissa B — Block 2-4: Qism #3 — اسمائے اشارہ ka taaruf

Section 13 mein humne **Ism Ghair Mutamakkin ki 8 qismein** dekhi thi. Qism #1 (Mudmaraat) Section 13-15 par mukammal hua. Qism #2 (Mausoolah) Section 15 + Section 16 Hissa A par. Ab **Qism #3 — اسمائے اشارہ** start hota.

**Ishara ka literal matlab** *(meri taraf se etymology; book ne explicit nahi diya)*: إِشَارَۃ = "ishara karna" / "pointing". Ism Ishara = "pointer pronoun" — kisi cheez ya shakhs ki taraf **ishara karne wala**.

**2 sub-qismein** (Block 2 PDF):
1. **اِشَارَہ قَرِیْب** (Ishara Qareeb) — "close pointing" — paas wale ke liye (roughly English "this/these")
2. **اِشَارَہ بَعِیْد** (Ishara Ba'eed) — "far pointing" — door wale ke liye (roughly English "that/those")

**Total 10 forms** = 5 Qareeb + 5 Ba'eed.

**Ishara Qareeb (Block 3)**:

| **هَاتَانِ** | یہ دو عورتیں | F dual |
| **هٰؤُلَاءِ** | یہ سب مرد یا یہ سب عورتیں | Plural — gender-common (m OR f) |

**Ishara Ba'eed (Block 4)**:

**Cross-table pattern** *(meri taraf se observation; book ne yeh tabseera nahi diya)*:

| Gender/Number | Qareeb | Ba'eed | Pattern |

| M sing | هٰذَا | ذٰلِكَ | Qareeb: **هٰ-** prefix; Ba'eed: ends in **-كَ** |

#### Hissa C — Block 5 Faida: مشار الیہ ki poori tareef

Section 12 (p-17) mein **مشار الیہ** ka **naam** aaya tha as Mushabahat ki tareeqa 2 mein (Asma-e-Ishara ki "احتیاج" — Mushar Ilayh chahiye). **Yahaan poori tareef** finally milti:

> **مشار الیہ = "Jis ki taraf ishara کیا جائے"** (Block 5 PDF)

Yaani **wo cheez ya shakhs** jis ki taraf Ism Ishara point kar raha.

**Book ki misaal**: **هٰذَا الْقَلَمُ نَفِیْسٌ** = "Yeh qalam qeemati hai"

| Component | Role |
|-----------|------|
| **هٰذَا** | Ism Ishara ("yeh") |
| **الْقَلَمُ** | **مشار الیہ** (jis ki taraf "yeh" point kar raha — qalam) |
| **نَفِیْسٌ** | Khabar (qeemati hai) |

**سلہ ↔ مشار الیہ pattern** *(meri taraf se observation)*: Jaisa **Mausool ke saath سلہ** lazmi (Section 15), waise **Ism Ishara ke saath مشار الیہ** lazmi. Dono cases mein "compound unit" ka pattern — akela kaam nahi chalta. Yeh **Section 12 ki Mushabahat tareeqa 2** ka practical demonstration hai: dono qismein ki "احتیاج" (need-based dependency) ab clear.

#### Hissa D — Block 6 Tarkeeb walkthrough (هٰذَا الْقَلَمُ نَفِیْسٌ)

Book ne 5-step tarkeeb di:

| Step | Component | Role (PDF verbatim) |
|------|-----------|----------------------|
| 1 | **هٰذَا** | اسم اشارہ |
| 2 | **الْقَلَمُ** | مشار الیہ |
| 3 | (Ism Ishara + Mushar Ilayh) | **مل کر مبتدا** |
| 4 | **نَفِیْسٌ** | خبر |

**Mausool walkthrough ke saath comparison** *(meri taraf se observation; book ne explicit comparison nahi kiya)*: Section 15 (p-20) ki **Mausool tarkeeb** mein 4-layer pattern tha — Mausool + Sila mil kar Fa'il, phir Fi'l + Fa'il mil kar Jumla Fi'liya. Yahaan **Ishara + Mushar Ilayh** mil kar Mubtada, phir Mubtada + Khabar mil kar Jumla Ismiya. **Same compound-unit pattern**, alag jumla type (Fi'liya vs Ismiya).

#### Hissa E — Block 7 Exercise 2 (Ishara ke 5 tarkeeb jumlay)

Book aakhir mein 5 practice jumlay deti hai Ishara ke saath:

1. **هٰذَا الْبَیْتُ قَدِیْمٌ** — "Yeh ghar purana hai"
   - هٰذَا (Ishara Qareeb m sing) + الْبَیْتُ (Mushar Ilayh) = Mubtada; قَدِیْمٌ = Khabar
2. **هٰؤُلَاءِ إِخْوَانُ سَعِیْدٍ** — "Yeh sab Sa'eed ke bhai hain"
   - هٰؤُلَاءِ (Ishara Qareeb plural) + إِخْوَانُ (Mushar Ilayh — Mudaf, damma without tanween) + سَعِیْدٍ (Mudaf Ilayh — Majroor bi Idafa with kasratain, proper name)
3. **هٰذِهِ الْمَرْأَۃُ صَالِحَۃٌ** — "Yeh aurat naik hai"
   - هٰذِهِ (Ishara Qareeb f sing) + الْمَرْأَۃُ (Mushar Ilayh) = Mubtada; صَالِحَۃٌ = Khabar
4. **هَاتَانِ الْبِنْتَانِ أُخْتَانِ** — "Yeh dono ladkiyaan dono behnein hain"
   - هَاتَانِ (Ishara Qareeb f dual) + الْبِنْتَانِ (Mushar Ilayh dual); أُخْتَانِ = Khabar dual
5. **اُولٰئِكَ طُلَّابُ الْمَدْرَسَۃِ** — "Wo sab madrasa ke students hain"
   - اُولٰئِكَ (Ishara Ba'eed plural) + طُلَّابُ (Mushar Ilayh — Mudaf) + الْمَدْرَسَۃِ (Mudaf Ilayh — Majroor bi Idafa)

**Note** *(meri taraf se observation)*: Jumlay #2 aur #5 mein **layered tarkeeb** dikhti — Mushar Ilayh khud ek Mudaf hota hai, aur uske saath ek Mudaf Ilayh bhi. #1, #3, #4 mein simple direct Mushar Ilayh + Khabar.

### Yaad rakho (key points)

1. **Mausoolah ka practical (Block 1)** — 5 exercise jumlay dikhate hain ke **Mausool+Sila ka complete unit** alag-alag grammatical roles nibha sakta (Fa'il/Maf'ool/Majroor); inner constant, outer badalta.
2. **Asma-e-Ishara (Qism #3 of 8) ka taaruf** — **2 sub-qismein**: Qareeb (paas) + Ba'eed (door).
3. **Ishara Qareeb 5 forms**: هٰذَا (m sing), هٰذَانِ (m dual), هٰذِهِ (f sing), هَاتَانِ (f dual), هٰؤُلَاءِ (plural gender-common).
4. **Ishara Ba'eed 5 forms**: ذٰلِكَ (m sing), ذَانِكَ (m dual), تِلْكَ (f sing), تَانِكَ (f dual), اُولٰئِكَ (plural gender-common).
5. **Total Ishara = 10 forms**. Plural form (#5 in both tables) gender-common.
6. **Cross-pattern** *(mera observation)*: Qareeb usually **هٰ-** prefix; Ba'eed usually ends in **-كَ**.
